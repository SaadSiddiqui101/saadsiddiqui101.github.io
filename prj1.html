Designing a scalable data-driven enterprise application

Introduction

There are more than 2824 products currently being exported to 194 countries, and 4039 products are being imported from 208 countries, making import and export a massive ecosystem that includes traders, banks, and 73 federal ministries that regulate the import and export.

Pakistan Single Window is an enterprise that set out to build a system that would improve the efficiency of Pakistan’s Trade System and digitize various government and federal ministries. It will comply with the Single Window standards of other countries and provide a single gateway for trade-related activities.

As a User Experience Engineer my goal was to design a modular system to help the team ideate and develop most of PSW's functions with ease and efficiency.

Why did we need a modular design system?

Consistency

For documenting our design decisions so we could reflect on them and try to remove inconsistencies.

Scalability

For quick expansion without requiring lots of effort. Our team could use pre-existing patterns to build, test, and deploy functionality.

Efficiency

For brainstorming and building, the process becomes much more efficient when there are predefined patterns than can be used by the team.

How did we start our journey?

Mapping the architecture

Initially, we created an information architecture to map out the main functions. This activity gave us an insight into the scale and user flows.

A structural overview of PSW's main functions

Working with Requirement Artifacts

After analyzing requirement artifacts like business requirements, and requirement traceability matrices, we improved the user flow, ensuring they align with the business and product goals.

Auditing Component Library and Removing Inconsistencies

After understanding the scale, functions, and user flows, we audited the existing component library and fixed any inconsistent design decisions.

The Design

Visual Library & Patterns

    PSW Branding contains two main colors in its palette, Lapis Lazuli (#3E5277) and Green (#009A5E). The palette exists, keeping accessibility in mind. (The shades are easily distinguishable even in the case of color blindness).

    Two fonts have been introduced as part of PSW’s branding. These two fonts are Proxima Nova & Montreal. The font pairing provides a good weight variation while keeping the overall visual style consistent.

    Predefined layouts are designed focusing on the data. These layouts help in quickly arranging large chunks of data on the screen.
