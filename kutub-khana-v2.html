elvey picklepants

Helping freshmen get course material without spending too much

Introduction

To provide structural education, many universities in Pakistan make buying course books necessary. There are many formats in which these books are available, offering a thorough knowledge of a subject and also helping teachers in planning. They are available in stores, libraries, and various digital hubs at different costs.

Acquiring these books presents itself with a few problems which discourage students. To empathize and understand these problems and come up with a solution for them, I conducted interview sessions and decided to come up with a solution following a three-phase design thinking framework.

Phase 1 - Research

To start our journey, I got in touch with university students for qualitative interviews and surveys. The first thing was to inquire about the need for hard-copies of books since students have other options like eBooks and PDFs etc.

During one of the interview, a statement I recieved was

"There is no need to buy a hard copy of the course material because there are PDFs available."

But a survey afterwards showed that not everyone was inclined towards this idea and to be precise, out of 30 respondents, 27 of the them have referred to the written course books once or more than one time during their studies. Furthermore, I also found out that the majority of people who were using the books were mostly freshmen and sophomore students and most of these books were used for only a small duration and for certain courses.

"I would choose books because it is much easier to focus. I use books for math related subjects such as Linear Algebra. It is much easier to read equations in printed format rather than on a screen."

Survey Results: Usage of course books.

Key Takeaways (Design Opportunities)

Two major challenges identified in this scenario were

Challenge 1: The Unavailability of books

During interviews we found out that most students were struggling to obtain course material when needed. The other option for the students was to issue the book from the library but they faced the same issue there as well.

"Most of the books are not available in the stores nearby, and you have to go far in order to buy them."

Challenge 2: The Feeling of being wasteful with money

Upon further inquiry, most of the students in third and fourth year of their bachelors program told us that the books they bought in the first year were still with them and that those books were of no use to them and they felt that they wasted their money.

"Many of the course books are expensive. Also why should we buy books when they are only going to be used for one semester and then kept away."
