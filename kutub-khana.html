elvey picklepants

Helping freshmen get course material without spending too much

Introduction

To provide structural education, many universities in Pakistan make buying course books necessary. There are many formats in which these books are available, offering a thorough knowledge of a subject and also helping teachers in planning. They are available in stores, libraries, and various digital hubs at different costs.

Acquiring these books presents itself with a few problems which discourage students. To empathize and understand these problems and come up with a solution for them, I conducted interview sessions and decided to come up with a solution following a three-phase design thinking framework.

Phase 1 - Research

To start our journey, I got in touch with university students for qualitative interviews and surveys. A statement I received while trying to dive deeper into this problem was

"There is no need to buy a hard copy of the course material because there are PDFs available."

But after doing a survey I found out that not everyone was inclined towards this idea and to be precise, out of 30 respondents, 27 of the them have referred to the written course books once or more than one time during their studies.

Furthermore, I also found out that the majority of people who were using the books were mostly freshmen and sophomore students.

"Most of the students in the third and fourth year of their bachelor's program told us that the books they bought in the first year were still with them and that those books were of no use to them and they felt that they wasted their money."
