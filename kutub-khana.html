Designing a scalable data-driven enterprise application

Introduction

There are more than 2824 products currently being exported to 194 countries, and 4039 products are being imported from 208 countries, making import and export a massive ecosystem that includes traders, banks, and 73 federal ministries that regulate the import and export.

Pakistan Single Window is an enterprise that set out to build a system that would improve the efficiency of Pakistanâ€™s Trade System and digitize various government and federal ministries. It will comply with the Single Window standards of other countries and provide a single gateway for trade-related activities.

As a User Experience Engineer my goal was to design a modular system to help the team ideate and develop most of PSW's functions with ease and efficiency.

Why did we need a modular design system?

Consistency

For documenting our design decisions so we could reflect on them and try to remove inconsistencies.

Scalability

For quick expansion without requiring lots of effort. Our team could use pre-existing patterns to build, test, and deploy functionality.

Efficiency

For brainstorming and building, the process becomes much more efficient when there are predefined patterns than can be used by the team.

How did we start our journey?

Mapping the architecture

Initially, we created an information architecture to map out the main functions. This activity gave us an insight into the scale and user flows.

A structural overview of PSW's main functions

Working with Requirement Artifacts

After analyzing requirement artifacts like business requirements, and requirement traceability matrices, we improved the user flow, ensuring they align with the business and product goals.

Auditing Component Library and Removing Inconsistencies

After understanding the scale, functions, and user flows, we audited the existing component library and fixed any inconsistent design decisions.

At the end of identification and research phase, we identified following goals

To provide a consistent experience across all modules and all platforms.

Since Pakistan Single Window (PSW) Portal supports multiple platforms and encompasses various functionalities, it was crucial to understand the need for a consistent experience.

To make the experience adaptable to data

One of the challenges identified was adapting the experience to changes in data. This meant considering better empty states in the absence of data, but resizable and easily navigable components when there is lots of data.

To reduce the learning curve of the traders new to the digital platform

Our target audience consisted of people who have been doing import and export related activities manually. This meant that they were not well-versed with using a digital system as a replacement. Reducing the learning curve and making it easier for users to understand and perform actions was also a challenge.

The Design

Below are some of the primary UI patterns designed to help the team achieve the goal.

    The design system contains two main colors in its palette, Lapis Lazuli (#3E5277) and Green (#009A5E). The palette exists, keeping accessibility in mind. (The shades are easily distinguishable even in the case of color blindness).

    Two fonts have been introduced as part of the design system. These two fonts are Proxima Nova & Montreal. The font pairing provides a good weight variation while keeping the overall visual style consistent.

    Predefined layouts are designed focusing on the data. These layouts help in quickly arranging large chunks of data on the screen.

    Form submission is one of the most frequent actions in the application. These forms are the exact conversion of the paper-based forms with data items required to be filled by the traders. Some of the major parts of the form pattern includes hierarchy of actions through visual separation and sub-forms.

    We ensured that the grids displaying the huge amount of data were easily navigable by introducing various sub-functions like search, sort, collapse, lock etc.
